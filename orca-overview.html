ORCA Overview

An integrated five-stage recycling loop that captures orbital debris and transforms it into useful space infrastructure

Solution & Technology: ORCA's 5-Stage Loop

ORCA's solution is an integrated five-stage recycling loop that captures orbital debris and transforms it into useful space infrastructure. This closed-loop system actively cleans up orbital debris while turning it into new orbital assets within a single platform.

Stage 1: Capture

Specialized ORCA satellites retrieve debris using different approaches for different sizes of objects.

Small Debris Capture

1

Micro-Debris Drones

Agile micro-debris capture drones handle centimeter-scale debris up to pieces about the size of a basketball

2

Net & Cone Collectors

Use nets or sticky "cone" collectors to snag clusters of small junk efficiently

3

On-Spot Processing

Micro-drones can perform initial processing on the spot for small debris pieces

Large Debris Capture

1

Robust Tug Satellites

Handle large debris like old satellites or spent rocket bodies weighing hundreds to thousands of kg

2

Robotic Arms & Claws

Use robotic arms or claw-like mechanisms to grapple large objects securely

3

Gentle Towing

Tugs gently tow large debris back to the hub with controlled movements to prevent fragmentation

Stage 2: Vacuum Bakeout

Once debris is secured at the hub, it undergoes a bakeout/decontamination step in a vacuum chamber to prepare it for processing.

Bakeout Process

1

Controlled Heating

Debris is slowly heated in vacuum to boil off volatile substances and leftover fuel

2

Coating Removal

Remove non-metallic coatings and dry out epoxies or plastics

3

Stabilization

Eliminate risk of pressure buildup or explosions, stabilizing the scrap material

Benefits

Safety

Prevents explosions and pressure buildup from volatile materials

Purity

Removes contaminants and coatings that could affect metal quality

Preparation

Prepares debris for efficient melting and processing in subsequent stages

Stage 3: Electron-Beam Cold-Hearth Melting & Alloy Refinement

The core of ORCA's hub is an electron beam furnace operating in vacuum. This stage transforms captured metals into purified molten metal suitable for manufacturing.

Melting Process

1

Electron Beam Heating

Electron beam heats and melts the scrap metal in a vacuum environment

2

Electromagnetic Separation

Use electromagnetic fields and special skimming techniques to remove slag and impurities

3

Alloy Blending

Different debris metals can be blended or refined to achieve usable new alloys

Technical Innovation

Microgravity Advantage

Molten metal doesn't separate by weight like on Earth, allowing precise control

Vacuum Operation

Electron-beam melting thrives in vacuum, producing high-quality ingots

Quality Control

Produces uniform material feedstock despite varied mix of incoming scrap

Stage 4: Wire Draw & Spool

The refined molten metal is cast and drawn into wire feedstock, creating standardized filament for 3D printing.

Wire Production Process

1

Extrusion

Metal is extruded through dies to form thin wire (1–2 mm diameter)

2

Cooling & Solidification

Wire is cooled and wound onto spools under tension to ensure proper solidification in zero G

3

Spooling

Creates reels of high-grade metal wire from what used to be orbital debris

Benefits

Standardization

Creates standardized filament that serves as raw material for 3D printing

Efficiency

Wire feedstock is efficient for space printing operations

Management

Tiny bits of shrapnel become neat, manageable feedstock rather than loose pieces

Stage 5: Electron Beam Additive Manufacturing (EBAM) & Deployment

In the final stage, the spooled wire is fed into large-format 3D printers using electron-beam additive manufacturing to fabricate new components.

Manufacturing Process

1

Wire Feeding

Spooled wire is fed into the hub's large-format 3D printers

2

Layer-by-Layer Building

Robotic printers weld-deposit metal wire layer by layer to fabricate components

3

Component Production

Print satellite structural parts, truss beams, panels, tools, or entire small satellites

4

Closed-Loop Recycling

Leftover metal scraps from printing are collected and fed back into Stage 3 for re-melting

Deployment Options

Direct Deployment

Deploy finished products directly in orbit (e.g., attach printed panel to space station)

Customer Delivery

Deliver products to customers via tug vehicles

Zero Waste

Closed-loop process with essentially zero waste through recycling of scraps

Technical Innovation & Robustness

ORCA's 5-stage process is designed specifically for microgravity and vacuum, taking advantage of the space environment while ensuring reliability and scalability.

Space Environment Advantages

Natural Vacuum

Space vacuum makes outgassing easier and is ideal for electron-beam operations

Microgravity Benefits

Electromagnetic fields can position and stir melts, compensating for absence of buoyancy

Energy Efficiency

No need for vacuum pumps, reducing energy requirements and complexity

Modular & Redundant Design

1

Multiple Chambers

Hub contains multiple furnace chambers and printer bays for redundancy

2

Distributed Fleet

Many drones and tugs ensure no single failure cripples the whole operation

3

Scalable Architecture

Can scale up by adding more units (capture satellites or processing modules)

Proven Technology Foundation

ISS Validation

Builds on proven principles from prior ISS metal printing tests

Industrial Adaptation

Adapts proven industrial processes into orbital workshop environment

Integrated Innovation

Achieves breakthrough by integrating technologies into single closed-loop system

Core Technologies

ORCA integrates three key technologies that work together to create the complete recycling and manufacturing system.

1. Electron-Beam Cold-Hearth Melting (EBCHM)

Purpose

Refine and purify mixed-metal debris into uniform, high-quality alloys

Electron Beam

20–60 kilovolts operating voltage for focused electron beam generation

Cold-Hearth Design

Water-cooled copper hearth prevents contamination from crucible materials

Refinement Process

Dense inclusions sink, lighter impurities float as slag for removal

2. Electron-Beam Additive Manufacturing (EBAM)

Purpose

Enable in-space 3D printing of structural metal components

Feedstock

1–2 mm diameter wire continuously supplied to print head

Process

Electron beam melts wire tip and deposits molten metal layer by layer

Quality

Near-net-shape, dense components with minimal post-processing

3. Gecko-Inspired Adhesive Grippers

Purpose

Capture small or irregularly shaped debris without suction, magnets, or mechanical force

Design

Millions of synthetic microfibers mimicking gecko feet setae

Mechanism

Van der Waals forces create strong adhesive bond with directional release

Validation

Tested on ISS by NASA JPL and Stanford University

System Integration

The entire ORCA process, from capture all the way through printing, is modular and repeatable. By integrating these technologies into one closed-loop system, ORCA achieves a real breakthrough: actively cleaning up orbital debris and turning it into new orbital assets within a single platform.

Integration Benefits

Complete Solution

Single platform handles entire lifecycle from debris capture to finished products

Closed-Loop Efficiency

Zero waste system with continuous recycling of materials

Scalable Operations

Modular design allows for expansion and replication across multiple orbital locations

Mission Impact

1

Debris Cleanup

Actively removes orbital debris, reducing collision risks and space pollution

2

Resource Creation

Transforms waste into valuable materials for space infrastructure

3

Sustainable Economy

Enables self-sustaining orbital economy with reduced dependence on Earth launches
