ORCA Development Plan

A comprehensive roadmap for transforming orbital debris into sustainable infrastructure

Three-Phase Development Strategy

ORCA will be developed and rolled out in three major phases, with a clear roadmap of technical milestones, funding needs, and business growth at each stage. Each phase has specific goals and go/no-go checkpoints to manage risk and demonstrate viability before moving to the next stage. In Phase 1 we prove the core technology, in Phase 2 we prove the business on a limited scale, and in Phase 3 we scale up to full operations.

Phase 1: Proof-of-Concept (2025–2029)

Estimated Cost

$5–10 Million

Duration

4 years (2025-2029)

Primary Focus

R&D and early prototyping

Key Objectives

1

Lab Demonstrations

Conduct ground-based testing of debris capture technologies, including net capture systems and mini-furnace units with simulated debris.

2

Microgravity Experiments

Perform melting and printing process experiments on parabolic flights or small payloads on the ISS to validate zero-gravity operations.

3

CubeSat Demo Mission

Launch a small "seed factory" CubeSat by 2028 to capture a test object and 3D-print a simple part in orbit, validating core technology.

Funding Sources

Research Grants

NASA SBIR grants, ESA technology development programs

Seed Investments

Early-stage investor funding for concept validation

Demo Contracts

Small agency contracts for demonstration missions

Phase 2: ORCA Pilot Hub Deployment (2030–2035)

Estimated Cost

$100–250 Million

Duration

5 years (2030-2035)

Primary Focus

Full-scale implementation in LEO

Key Milestones

1

First ORCA Hub Launch (2032)

Deploy the first full ORCA hub including processing module, furnace, robotic arms, and all core systems.

2

Fleet Deployment

Launch one or two large tug satellites and a fleet of micro-debris drones to support hub operations.

3

End-to-End Demo (2033)

Demonstrate complete operations: capture actual orbital debris, refine the metal, and 3D-print usable structures like truss segments or panels.

4

Commercial Operations

Begin service operations for paying customers including debris removal contracts and printing jobs for clients.

Technology Enhancements

AI-Based Navigation

Implement autonomous drone navigation systems for efficient debris capture

Automated Self-Repair

Develop self-repair capabilities for the hub to maintain operations

Capacity Scaling

Ramp up processing capacity through 2035 based on operational experience

Funding Sources

Government Contracts

NASA and DoD debris removal missions, ESA Zero Debris initiatives

Venture Capital

Series A/B funding rounds for major infrastructure investment

Strategic Partnerships

Corporate partners investing in or co-funding the project

Phase 3: Scaled Operations & Expansion (2036–2045)

Estimated Cost

$300–500 Million

Duration

9 years (2036-2045)

Primary Focus

Orbital construction yard operations

Expansion Strategy

1

Multi-Hub Operations

Enhance the original LEO hub and deploy a second hub in different orbits (different LEO inclination or GEO).

2

Full Commercialization

Serve multiple customers routinely, supplying material and performing on-orbit fabrication as regular service.

3

New Revenue Models

Introduce subscription plans and joint ventures with commercial space station operators.

4

Large-Scale Construction

Take on major projects like building structures for solar power satellites entirely in orbit.

Financial Milestones

IPO Preparation

Prepare for major financial events like taking the company public

Lunar Expansion

Establish presence in lunar orbit for future space economy

Self-Sustaining Operations

Fund growth through reinvested profits rather than external funding

Financial Projections

Based on this phased approach, ORCA's financial plan shows an initial period of heavy investment and negative cash flow (during Phase 1 and Phase 2 when we are doing R&D and building everything) followed by breakeven and profitability by mid-Phase 2.

Break-Even Timeline

1

Phase 1: R&D Investment

Most expenses are R&D (salaries, prototype development) with little to no revenue aside from grants. Normal startup operating loss.

2

Phase 2: Capital & Initial Revenue

Major capital expenditures for hub construction and launches, but initial revenues in millions per year from contracts. Target break-even by 2033-2035.

3

Phase 3: Scale & Profitability

Revenues scale to hundreds of millions as ORCA becomes an orbital services provider, with costs growing more slowly due to efficiencies.

Unit Economics

ORCA's service is compelling on a per-unit economic basis. Producing material in orbit via ORCA is estimated to cost about $2,000 per kg, whereas launching material from Earth costs around $10,000 per kg.

5× Cost Advantage

ORCA can charge customers well below Earth-launch prices while maintaining healthy margins

Dual Revenue Stream

Monetize debris twice: get paid to remove it, then sell the recycled product made from that debris

Collision Prevention Value

Preventing a single $200 million satellite collision more than justifies ORCA's services

Prototype Integration & Impact

To validate the ORCA concept, our team developed an integrated prototype demonstration (as part of the NASA Space Apps Challenge) that brings the idea to life on a small scale.

Prototype Components

1

Physical Models

3D-printed scale models of micro-debris capture satellites and large-debris tugs with net-cone collectors and claw-like arms.

2

Digital Simulation

Built with CesiumJS and NASA's orbital debris data to visualize Earth orbit with real debris objects in real-time.

3

Animated Mission Sequence

End-to-end animation showing debris capture, processing, melting, 3D-printing, and deployment of new structures.

4

Interactive Dashboard

Real-time metrics showing debris mass recycled, launch costs saved, and new parts fabricated with gamified feedback.

Key Assumptions

In developing this business plan and our projections, we made several key assumptions that underpin our financial and technical projections.

Launch Cost to LEO

$10,000 per kg average cost for payload launches to LEO

ORCA Recycling Cost

$2,000 per kg effective cost for capture, processing, and production in orbit

Market Size (TAM)

$100 billion by 2040s for debris removal and in-space manufacturing

Serviceable Market (SAM)

$5 billion by 2030s for LEO-focused services ORCA can realistically serve

Target Market (SOM)

$0.5 billion revenue target by 2035, capturing ~10% of SAM

Recycling Efficiency

>90% of metal content from captured debris can be converted to usable feedstock

Technology Readiness

Key technologies can be demonstrated by late 2020s and scaled by early 2030s

Regulatory Environment

Supportive regulatory framework for private debris removal and recycling operations
