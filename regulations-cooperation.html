Regulations & International Cooperation

Navigating complex international regulations and upholding high ethical standards for space activities

Our Approach

Operating an orbital recycling venture means navigating complex international regulations and upholding high ethical standards for space activities. ORCA has been proactive in building compliance and cooperation into its model from day one.

Debris Ownership & Legal Framework

Under the Outer Space Treaty, space objects remain the property of their launching state, which means ORCA cannot just go and take debris without permission – legally, even a defunct satellite still "belongs" to someone.

Legal Compliance Strategy

1

Agreement & Licensing

Secure agreements or licenses for each high-value object we plan to remove, obtaining consent from the object's state via the U.N. space object registry.

2

Targeted Missions

Early missions specifically target debris that is clearly abandoned and non-functional, carried out under contracts with agencies like NASA or ESA.

3

Policy Development

Actively contribute to evolving policy discussions on space salvage rights, helping shape frameworks that treat debris removal and recycling as a public good.

4

Export Control Compliance

Adhere to all export control and ITAR regulations, safeguarding sensitive technology like capture mechanisms and furnace designs.

Security & Technology Protection

Controlled Technology

Sensitive tech like capture mechanisms and furnace designs are safeguarded according to national security restrictions

International Partnerships

Structured to respect national security restrictions and protect any controlled technologies

Legal Cover

Ensure we have legal cover and diplomatic backing for all operations

International Cooperation & Partnerships

ORCA's mission is inherently global – space debris doesn't respect any borders. We are forging partnerships with space agencies and companies around the world.

Global Partnerships

1

NASA & ESA Collaboration

Team up with NASA and ESA on pilot clean-up missions, leveraging their funding and technical resources for initial operations.

2

International Agency Cooperation

Collaborate with JAXA (Japan), ISRO (India), and CNSA (China) on sharing debris data and technology lessons.

3

Commercial Partnerships

Work with commercial partners like Axiom Space or Northrop Grumman to integrate ORCA capabilities into future space stations.

4

Competitor Collaboration

Partner with entities like Astroscale or ClearSpace to focus on capturing debris and hand it off to ORCA for recycling instead of deorbiting.

Ecosystem Development

Inclusive Platform

Position ORCA as an inclusive platform encouraging others to contribute debris or buy materials

Win-Win Ecosystem

Create collaborative relationships rather than competitive ones in the debris removal space

Transparency & Ethical Operations

ORCA is committed to complete transparency in our activities and ethical operations that set the gold standard for space businesses.

Transparency Measures

1

Public Registry

Maintain a public orbital debris recycling registry with an online dashboard listing every piece of debris captured by catalog ID.

2

Impact Reports

Release regular reports detailing total tons of debris cleared and estimated reduction in collision risk due to our operations.

3

Trust Building

Build trust with regulators, customers, and the general public by showing ORCA as a responsible steward of orbit.

Ethical Commitments

Do No Harm

All operations designed to avoid creating new debris or any other negative side effects

Controlled Disposal

Deorbit any ORCA components that reach end-of-life in a controlled manner, complying with the 5-year rule

Collaborative Safety

Share orbital tracking data and coordinate with organizations like U.S. Space Command

Environmental Stewardship

1

Core Mission Objective

Treat orbital environmental sustainability as a core mission objective, not just a convenient byproduct

2

Interference Prevention

Ensure our activities don't interfere with active spacecraft through coordination and data sharing

3

Responsible Operations

Maintain the highest standards of responsible space operations and environmental protection
