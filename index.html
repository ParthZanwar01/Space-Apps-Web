1. Electron-Beam Cold-Hearth Melting (EBCHM)

Electron-beam cold-hearth melting is used to refine and purify mixed-metal debris collected from orbit into uniform, high-quality alloys suitable for manufacturing.

How It Works:

  • Electron Beam Generation: High-voltage electron gun (20–60 kilovolts) emits focused electrons in vacuum
  • Cold-Hearth Design: Water-cooled copper hearth prevents contamination
  • Refinement Process: Molten pool separates contaminants - dense inclusions sink, light impurities become slag
  • Space Optimization: Natural vacuum eliminates need for pumps, prevents oxidation

Space Applications:

  • Refines scrap metal into aerospace-grade alloys
  • Electromagnetic fields position and stir melt in microgravity
  • Produces materials for in-orbit manufacturing

2. Electron-Beam Additive Manufacturing (EBAM)

Electron-beam additive manufacturing enables in-space 3D printing of structural metal components using wire feedstock produced from refined debris.

How It Works:

  • Feedstock Delivery: Wire feed (1–2 mm diameter) supplied into print head
  • Melting and Deposition: Electron beam melts wire tip and deposits molten metal
  • Layer Formation: Robotic gantry builds part layer by layer with real-time sensors
  • Result: Near-net-shape, dense components requiring minimal post-processing

Space Applications:

  • Eliminates need to launch bulky structures from Earth
  • Fabricates trusses, panels, satellite frames in orbit
  • Uses reclaimed materials for sustainable manufacturing
  • Based on NASA's Electron Beam Freeform Fabrication (EBF³)

3. Gecko-Inspired Adhesive Grippers

Gecko-inspired adhesive grippers allow ORCA's micro-debris drones to capture small or irregularly shaped debris without using suction, magnets, or mechanical force.

How It Works:

  • Microstructure Design: Synthetic microfibers mimic gecko setae
  • Adhesion Mechanism: Van der Waals forces create strong molecular bonds
  • Control and Release: Directional adhesion resists shear but releases when peeled
  • Gentle Contact: Secure attachment without significant pressure

Space Applications:

  • Captures varied materials and geometries of orbital debris
  • Clean, reusable, non-destructive attachment method
  • Tested on International Space Station by NASA JPL and Stanford
  • Essential for microgravity debris collection
