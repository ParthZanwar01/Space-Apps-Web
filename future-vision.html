Future Vision

Envisioning a self-sustaining orbital economy where space debris becomes a valuable resource

Our Vision

Our vision is a future where space debris isn't just a threat but a useful resource, enabling a self-sustaining orbital economy. ORCA's approach to scalability and sustainability not only prevents the orbital debris problem from getting worse, it actually turns the solution into a resilient orbital infrastructure.

Core Vision Elements

Resource Transformation

Convert space debris from threat to valuable resource for orbital infrastructure

Self-Sustaining Economy

Create a circular economy in space that reduces dependence on Earth launches

Infrastructure Backbone

Become the "orbital infrastructure backbone" supporting space exploration and development

Future Markets & Expansion

Later on, we anticipate serving GEO satellite operators (once ORCA extends to geostationary orbits) and even research or tourism outposts in cislunar space (lunar orbit stations) when those emerge. Basically, as we expand beyond LEO, our customer base can grow to anyone operating in those new frontiers.

Market Expansion Timeline

1

LEO Operations (2030-2035)

Establish first ORCA hub in Low Earth Orbit, serving LEO satellite operators and space stations

2

GEO Expansion (2035-2040)

Deploy hubs to geostationary orbit to serve GEO satellite operators and high-altitude missions

3

Cislunar Operations (2040-2045)

Extend to cislunar space to support lunar orbit stations, research outposts, and tourism facilities

4

Deep Space Support (2045+)

Provide materials and services for missions beyond Earth-Moon system and interplanetary exploration

Customer Base Growth

Research Outposts

Support scientific research facilities in cislunar space with materials and services

Tourism Facilities

Provide infrastructure support for space tourism and commercial space stations

Network Development

We plan to deploy additional ORCA hubs to geostationary orbit (GEO) and even to cislunar space (lunar orbits) to service satellites and stations there. The modular architecture means we can add new processing modules or printer bays to an existing hub, or launch a clone of the hub to a different orbital inclination or altitude.

Network Evolution

1

First ORCA hub operational in LEO, proving the concept and establishing operations

2

3

Sustainable Supply Chain

Create a sustainable supply chain beyond Earth using recycled orbital materials

Regulatory Environment

We assume there won't be new laws or regulations that flat-out prohibit private companies from removing or modifying debris by the time ORCA is ready to operate. In fact, we expect the opposite – that international consensus on debris remediation will improve access for ventures like ORCA.

Regulatory Assumptions

Supportive Framework

International consensus on debris remediation will improve access for private ventures

Policy Evolution

Regulatory frameworks will evolve to support active debris removal and recycling

Collaborative Approach

Work with regulators to shape policies that enable sustainable space operations
